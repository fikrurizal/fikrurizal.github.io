My research examines how economic conditions and health-system design affect health, healthcare use, and financial protection. I focus especially on low- and middle-income country settings, with an emphasis on Indonesia and Southeast Asia.

## Themes

* **Inequality and access:** socioeconomic gradients in health, healthcare utilisation, and barriers to care.
* **Health financing:** insurance design, public financing, and financial protection.
* **Policy evaluation:** empirical evaluation of health policies and system reforms.
* **Economic determinants of health:** how income, employment, household circumstances, and local context shape health behaviour and outcomes.

## Methods

I use applied econometrics, health economic evaluation, systematic review methods, and policy analysis. My work is informed by clinical training, health economics, and experience working with health policymakers.

## Current Project

At Monash University, I am working on the *Connecting for Better Health* project with the Health Economics Group in the School of Public Health and Preventive Medicine.
